Hello, I'm Dr. Derek Ho, a marine mammal ecologist and conservation scientist at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University and the Cetacea Research Institute. My work focuses on understanding how human-caused stressors impact dolphins and porpoises, using data to find effective conservation solutions. 

My research is built on three core themes:
- Marine Mammal Ecology: I use tools like stable-isotope biogeochemistry in dolphin teeth to see how their diet has changed over their lives in response to environmental shifts.
- Underwater Soundscapes: I deploy underwater microphones (passive acoustic monitoring) and build machine-learning models to map where and when cetaceans are active and how they are affected by vessel noise.
- Microplastic Pollution: I quantify microplastic contamination—from rivers to marine animals—and study the microbial communities that live on these particles to assess ecological risks.

I then bring all this data together using Bayesian models to predict how ecosystems might change and to help guide effective, evidence-based management.

I'm committed to transparent, robust science through open-source code and data. My work has been published in journals like _Nature Communications_, _Journal of Hazardous Materials_ and _Biological Conservation_ etc. I earned my PhD (2021) and BSc (2013) from the University of Hong Kong.
